Reflect on this week's reading by Moulthrop. How does it speak to the internet today?

This week, I was tasked to read "You Say You Want a Revolution? Hypertext and the Laws of Media" by Stuart Moulthrop. The reading makes us aware of how the internet has revolutionized overtime, as we see that the internet relies on the concept of association where there is a link between information within its structure. This revolution results in a different way in which we approach reading and the consumption of information on the Internet, from a structure that was linear as it did not help us access information in ways that consumers could learn new things. This transition from the linear way that we consume information on the internet to a more interconnected structure draws from the theories of Marshall McLuhan and how the media has impacted society.

The reading takes influence from McLuhan, who emphasizes the concept of “Medium is the Message”. This concept thoroughly explains to us that hypertext as a new medium is what is crucial in how we consume information on the internet today. In last week’s reading, the term of “association” was brought up where understand that media is better consumed when interlinked with each other. The point was brought up that we learn and understand more information when it is linked to one another, and this allows us the opportunity to explore and understand new information that is linked to what we are looking for. This allows for a change in the way that one consumes information on the internet, where we are introduced to several paths on the internet that therefore results in the reader having interest in the information that they delve into while looking for content in the internet.

The way that the internet used to function was via traditional text, but hypertext diverges from this and as hypertext allows for internet consumers that are looking for information to navigate their way through different information and parts of the internet through what we call links. This also emphasizes on association, where we see that the idea of consuming information on the internet in a linear structure does not provide the opportunity for interaction and learning. What the hypertext does is provides the internet user an easier way to grasp and learn information, prioritizing the human interaction with technology. The hypertext makes sure that consumers of information have their own directions and links that they follow to further enhance the learner and reader experience.

This overall gives the reader and consumer a more personalized experience that will urge them to continue engaging with the information instead of passively consuming information in a way that would result in a lack of learning due a failed personal experience. The reading also makes the point of how hypertext as a navigation system on the internet can be pivotal for in the fight for social change. Moulthrop suggests this as hypertext can be a way that people can be exposed to new information raising awareness on social change and therefore allowing for more than one idea to thrive in the internet ecosystem, resulting in it being a place where different social ideas can thrive therefore, resulting in an educated society. Moulthrop uses the example of "Xanadu" which is a hpertext system used on the internet and how this system of hypertext is also an example of how technology and the internet has evolved today and how everything on the internet is interlinked to one another making information' easier to access.

Reflect on interaction and the WWW: What does interaction mean to me?

The word wide web is tool that we have been using for almost all of our lives and its functionality has been essential in our understanding of information and our journeys of learning new this and being informed about the things occurring in the world today. Through this experience, I have always appreciated just how easy it is to access information without having to search high and low for it and this is what is important especially for someone interacting with the World Wide Web, information at your fingertips at a quick time too.

When one interacts with the internet, it is important to understand that the process has to be one that functions so well to the extent that is almost hassle free where one can gain all the information that they need at a quick time all while being introduced to new things that link to what they are learning. With this, the process of interaction becomes one that is not only understandable but also very enjoyable.

When I interact with something, it first has to catch my eye immediately! If it successfully catches my eye, it gives me all of the reason to engage with it. This is why user interface is important for interaction as it serves at the attraction point that gives the user reason to be engaged. It is important to think about where you elements on your website will be, from your scroll and search bar and how people will click on information in order to access it. Think about how easy or sophisticated you want the interaction with the website to be and if this is beneficial for the website that you are developing. Your aim should be to satisfy your users as well as have a satisfiable and easy to navigate experience when working with a website and just like this, you’re a step further in enhancing one’s interaction experience with your website.

Which brings me to the next idea that I believe is important for someone who uses the internet, and an aspiring web developer is that the feedback that the website gives to users must be both effective and efficient. This means that when a user searches for something on the web, they must be able to find it and it must be made available to them in a way that helps the quickly. This feed back is essential in the user knowing what further steps to take and whether or not the website is responsive or not. Making the website flow to an extent that it is responsive and on time with feedback that it gives ensures that the user does not have any hassles when experiencing the website. This is why it is also important understand that as the users interact with your website, the build a connection with the website and therefore they either feel good or bad experiencing the website as a whole. Therefore this results in the user finding what they are looking for all the while enjoying what the website has to offer them.

Which brings us back to the beginning point, where we understand that the technical aspects of the website may be the ground work of it all, but if it isn’t an experience that one enjoys without any problems while having an experience that is enjoyable too, the website then falls short and affects the overall interactive experience which is why my goal overall is to create educational experiences all the while having a good experience navigating those educational experiences too.
